# WORLD-001 — Environment Design: Medical Wing

**Status:** Creative Studio deliverable — ready for EP review  
**Mission:** WORLD-001  
**Department:** Medical — Occupational Health & Clinical Observation  
**Authority:** Art Bible · Facility Bible · Design Plan  
**Unreal:** None — design intent only

---

## 1. Visual identity

Asterion Medical should read as a **real workplace clinic inside a classified research institute** — cleaner than Engineering, colder than Administration, quieter than Security.

| Axis | Direction |
|------|-----------|
| Tone | Clinical competence under institutional pressure |
| Contrast to Engineering | Less rust / oil; more scrubbed surfaces that still failed |
| Contrast to Research | Less glass science theater; more human-scale exam privacy |
| Contrast to Security | Soft partitions and curtains instead of ballistic glass; paperwork instead of cameras as primary residue |
| Horror register | Absence of care mid-procedure; isolation lights; unfinished charts — not gore set dressing |

**Brand test:** Without signage, the player should still know this is Medical from ceramic sinks, privacy curtains, exam lamps, chart rails, and PPE stations.

---

## 2. Architecture

### Massing & circulation

- Double-loaded corridor with **Clinical Threshold** as controlled entry from a Research-adjacent transfer hall.  
- Exam / Observation suites as side rooms with partial-height privacy + full-height hard walls for Isolation.  
- Records Alcove recessed (quiet, archival).  
- Pharmacy behind a half-dutch door / pass-through (controlled).  
- Occupational Health Console in a small glazed bay facing triage (visible authority).

### Dimensional intent (blockout scale)

| Space | Approx. footprint | Height | Notes |
|-------|-------------------|--------|-------|
| Transfer Corridor | 3.0 × 12–16 m | 3.0 m | Signage rhythm; clinical stripe |
| Clinical Threshold | 6 × 5 m | 3.0 m | Desk + waiting |
| Examination Suite A | 4 × 4.5 m | 2.8 m | Curtain + lamp |
| Observation Bay | 6 × 5 m | 3.0 m | 2 beds; isolation cue |
| Records Alcove | 3 × 3.5 m | 2.8 m | Cabinets dense |
| Pharmacy / Supply | 3.5 × 4 m | 2.8 m | Shelving + fridge silhouette |
| OH Console Bay | 3 × 3 m | 2.8 m | Terminal + printer |

### Architectural language

- Painted CMU / gypsum over concrete; **hospital-grade scrub wall** wainscot (matte seafoam / pale teal — desaturated).  
- Vinyl composition tile (VCT) with darkened joints; one cracked tile near Observation.  
- Acoustic drop ceiling with stained tiles above Observation (Engineering bleed — humidity story).  
- Corner guards, crash rails, handrails — institute-issue, slightly mismatched paint batches.  
- Door leaves: solid-core with narrow vision panels + privacy blinds (half drawn).

## 6. Furniture & fixtures

### Clinical Threshold

- Reception desk with badge tray, unfinished intake clipboard  
- 3–4 waiting chairs (one askew)  
- Magazine rack with institute newsletter (blanked/outdated)  
- Wall clock stopped or wrong shift time  

### Examination Suite A

- Exam table + paper roll half-used  
- Rolling stool; gooseneck exam lamp aimed at empty table  
- Wall otoscope / ophthalmoscope silhouettes (or generic diagnostic wall panel)  
- Privacy curtain 70% drawn  
- Sink + soap dispenser empty  

### Observation Bay

- Two beds; one stripped, one with folded blanket mid-fold  
- Bedside monitors dark / one soft standby LED  
- Isolation cart with PPE leftovers  
- Ceiling track for curtain between beds  

### Records Alcove

- Lateral file cabinets (one drawer open)  
- Chart rack; color tabs (Research misfile = env story)  
- Shred bin overflowing with unshredded coversheets  
- Desk with stamp set: `FIT` / `HOLD` / `REFER TO SECURITY`  

### Pharmacy / Supply

- Locked glass-front cabinet (seal broken)  
- Open shelving: saline, gauze, gloves  
- Small medical fridge (door ajar; warm light dead)  
- Sharps container overfull  

### OH Console Bay

- Desk + dual monitor / single terminal silhouette  
- Badge printer with jam  
- Wall board: shift roster half-erased  
- Desk phone off-hook or handset crooked

## 7. Prop storytelling priorities (read without text)

| Beat | Prop cluster | Reads as |
|------|--------------|----------|
| Care interrupted | Exam lamp on empty table; curtain drawn | Mid-exam evacuation |
| Institutional fear of symptoms | PPE cart staged at Observation door | Protocol over people |
| Research adjacency | Misfiled Research chart tab in Medical cabinet | Departments shared people |
| Security adjacency | Stack of clearance forms stamped HOLD | Fitness gates access |
| Supply stress | Pharmacy inventory sheet with red totals | Late-phase scarcity |
| Human scale | Personal mug in Records labeled with first name only | Staff were people |

---

## 8. Themes (creative pillars)

1. **Care as procedure** — medicine as institutional process, not mercy.  
2. **Fitness as control** — health used to gate movement (coheres with Security).  
3. **Observation without answers** — charts track symptoms; never resolve them.  
4. **Cleanliness that failed** — scrubbed surfaces cannot erase what happened.  
5. **Quiet adjacent to Research** — the human cost sits one corridor from the science.
