# Glossary

Status: Active

Version: 0.1

---

# Purpose

The Glossary defines the official terminology used throughout Project Echo.

This document is the canonical reference for names, locations, organizations, technologies, and supernatural phenomena.

All documentation, gameplay, notes, dialogue, UI text, and future chapters should follow these definitions.

If two documents conflict, the Glossary takes precedence for terminology.

---

# Organizations

## Asterion Research Institute

Type

Classified Research Facility

Description

The primary setting of Project Echo.

Originally established as a scientific research institute conducting advanced experimental studies.

The institute was abandoned following the events surrounding Project Echo.

Official Short Name

Asterion

---

## Project Echo

Classification

Top Secret Research Initiative

Description

The classified experiment conducted within Asterion Research Institute.

The true objectives of Project Echo remain unknown during the early game.

---

# Locations

## Reception

The first public area encountered by the player.

Acts as the introduction to the facility.

---

## Generator Room

Engineering area responsible for supplying emergency power.

Introduced during Milestone 1.

---

## Restricted Wing

Highly classified section of the institute.

Access requires advanced security clearance.

Not accessible during the early game.

---

# Characters

## The Player

Identity

Unknown

Description

The protagonist controlled by the player.

Their identity and connection to Asterion are intentionally hidden during the opening chapters.

---

## The Witness

Classification

Unknown

Description

The primary psychological threat within Project Echo.

The Witness represents uncertainty and fear of the unknown.

It should never be fully explained too early in the narrative.

---

# Events

## The Incident

Description

The catastrophic event that caused the collapse of normal operations at Asterion Research Institute.

Exact details are intentionally withheld during the early game.

---

## Power Restoration

Description

The first major environmental change initiated by the player.

Restoring power alters the facility and advances the narrative.

---

# Gameplay Terms

## Objective

A short-term goal presented to the player.

Objectives guide progression without breaking immersion.

---

## Interaction

Any player action performed through the Interaction System.

Examples include:

- Opening doors
- Reading notes
- Activating generators
- Collecting items

---

## Inventory Item

A gameplay object stored within the Inventory Component.

Examples:

- Facility Key
- Fuel Can

Future examples:

- Batteries
- Access Cards
- Fuses

---

# Technical Terms

## Blueprint Interface (BPI)

The preferred communication method between gameplay systems.

Used to reduce direct dependencies between Blueprints.

---

## Component

A reusable Blueprint module responsible for a specific gameplay system.

Examples:

- Interaction Component
- Flashlight Component
- Inventory Component
- Objective Component

---

## Event Dispatcher

A Blueprint event broadcasting mechanism used to notify multiple systems of important gameplay events.

Example:

Generator

↓

Power Restored

↓

Lights

Doors

Audio

Future AI
