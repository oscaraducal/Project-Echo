# Purpose

This document defines the visual identity of Project Echo.

Every environment, prop, material, and lighting decision should reinforce the atmosphere of psychological horror.

The goal is visual consistency rather than graphical complexity.

---

# Art Direction

Project Echo follows a grounded, realistic art style inspired by abandoned research facilities.

The environment should feel believable, lived-in, and unsettling.

Avoid exaggerated horror aesthetics.

---

# Visual Pillars

## Isolation

Rooms should feel empty.

Large spaces should emphasize loneliness.

---

## Decay

The facility has been abandoned.

Signs of age should include:

- Rust
- Water damage
- Cracked paint
- Dust
- Broken furniture
- Exposed wiring

Decay should tell a story.

---

## Contrast

Contrast creates tension.

Examples:

- Bright flashlight in complete darkness
- Clean laboratory beside destroyed hallway
- Emergency lights against deep shadows

---

## Environmental Storytelling

Every room should communicate its purpose without requiring text.

The player should recognize:

- Medical rooms
- Research labs
- Maintenance areas
- Offices
- Security checkpoints

through props and layout alone.

---

# Color Palette

Primary Colors

- Cold Gray
- Concrete White
- Steel Blue

Accent Colors

- Emergency Red
- Warning Yellow
- Industrial Orange

Lighting Colors

- White Fluorescent
- Soft Flashlight Warmth
- Red Emergency Lighting

Avoid overly saturated colors.

---

# Materials

Preferred Materials

- Concrete
- Steel
- Glass
- Ceramic
- Rubber
- Painted Metal

Surface Wear

- Rust
- Dirt
- Water stains
- Peeling paint
- Scratches

---

# Lighting Philosophy

Darkness is a gameplay mechanic.

The flashlight should be valuable.

Important objects should be subtly highlighted through composition rather than bright lights.

Emergency lighting should improve visibility without eliminating tension.

---

# Asset Guidelines

Every asset should satisfy at least one purpose:

- Gameplay
- Storytelling
- Navigation
- Atmosphere

Avoid decorative clutter without meaning.
