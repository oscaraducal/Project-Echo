# Purpose

This document defines the core gameplay loop of Project Echo.

Every new gameplay mechanic should reinforce this loop rather than replace it.

If a feature does not support this loop, it should be reconsidered.

# Detailed Breakdown

## 1. Explore

The player searches unfamiliar environments.

Goals:

- Discover new rooms
- Learn the facility layout
- Find useful objects
- Notice environmental details

Examples:

- Walking through abandoned hallways
- Entering maintenance rooms
- Searching storage areas

---

## 2. Observe

Players should pay attention before interacting.

Observation includes:

- Lighting
- Sounds
- Room condition
- Notes
- Locked doors
- Environmental clues

Good observation should reward careful players.

---

## 3. Interact

The player engages with the world.

Examples:

- Open doors
- Read notes
- Pick up items
- Activate generators
- Press switches
- Inspect machinery

Interactions should always have purpose.

---

## 4. Collect

Items enable progression.

Current examples:

- Flashlight
- Facility Key
- Fuel Can

Future examples:

- Batteries
- Fuse
- Access Cards
- Medical Keys

Collectibles should unlock gameplay rather than simply increase inventory.

---

## 5. Solve

Players overcome environmental problems.

Examples:

- Unlock a security door
- Restore power
- Find missing components
- Reactivate systems

Puzzles should emerge naturally from the environment.

---

## 6. Progress

Solving problems changes the world.

Examples:

- New rooms open
- Objectives update
- Power returns
- Environmental changes occur

The player should feel that their actions matter.

---

## 7. Reveal Story

Progress rewards the player with new information.

Story is revealed through:

- Notes
- Environmental storytelling
- Audio logs (future)
- Character encounters
- Room design

Story should encourage curiosity instead of forcing exposition.

---

## 8. Survive

Danger is a constant possibility.

The player must:

- Stay alert
- Listen carefully
- Manage resources
- Avoid unnecessary risks

Survival creates tension throughout exploration.

---

# Gameplay Principles

Every gameplay system should satisfy at least one of these goals:

- Increase tension
- Encourage exploration
- Reward curiosity
- Advance the narrative
- Deepen immersion

Avoid mechanics that exist only to add complexity.

# Progression Philosophy

Project Echo is not an RPG.

Progression comes from:

- Knowledge
- Exploration
- Access
- Story discoveries
- Environmental changes

The player becomes more capable through understanding the world rather than increasing numerical stats.
