# Narrative Design

Status: Active

Version: 0.1

---

# Purpose

This document defines how Project Echo tells its story.

The narrative should unfold naturally through player exploration, environmental storytelling, and meaningful discoveries rather than lengthy cutscenes or exposition.

---

# Storytelling Philosophy

Project Echo is a mystery.

Players are investigators rather than passive observers.

The game should encourage players to ask questions before providing answers.

---

# Narrative Delivery

Story is delivered through:

- Environmental storytelling
- Handwritten notes
- Research documents
- Audio logs (future)
- Visual clues
- Facility layout
- Character encounters

Cutscenes should be used sparingly.

---

# Narrative Pillars

## Mystery

Players should constantly wonder:

- What happened?
- Who caused it?
- What is Project Echo?
- What is The Witness?

---

## Discovery

Exploration is rewarded with information.

Hidden rooms should reveal meaningful lore rather than optional filler.

---

## Ambiguity

Not every question should be answered immediately.

Some mysteries should remain unresolved until later chapters.

---

## Consequence

The player's actions change the world.

Examples:

- Restoring power
- Unlocking restricted areas
- Activating security systems

These actions should have narrative as well as gameplay consequences.

---

# Writing Style

Dialogue and written notes should be:

- Concise
- Natural
- Believable
- Professional
- Free of unnecessary exposition

Every document should sound as though it was written by someone who worked at Asterion Research Institute.

---

# Tone

The narrative should feel:

- Isolated
- Unsettling
- Scientific
- Grounded
- Mysterious

Avoid melodrama or excessive horror clichés.
