# Audio Bible

Status: Active

Version: 0.1

---

# Purpose

Audio is one of the primary tools for creating fear in Project Echo.

Silence should be treated as an intentional design element.

---

# Audio Philosophy

The player should hear the facility before they understand it.

Sound builds anticipation.

Visuals confirm it.

---

# Ambient Audio

Every room should have a unique ambient identity.

Examples:

Reception

- HVAC hum
- Flickering fluorescent lights

Maintenance

- Metal vibration
- Water dripping

Laboratory

- Electrical buzzing
- Distant alarms

Generator Room

- Machinery
- Engine vibration

---

# Dynamic Audio

World events should change ambient sound.

Examples:

Generator starts

↓

Ventilation activates

↓

Electrical hum returns

↓

Emergency systems online

The environment should sound alive.

---

# Silence

Silence is powerful.

Use silence:

- Before major events
- Before Witness encounters
- After loud gameplay moments

Do not fill every space with sound.

---

# Music

Project Echo does not rely on constant background music.

Music should be reserved for:

- Major story moments
- Endings
- Important reveals

Atmospheric sound should carry most gameplay.

---

# The Witness

The Witness should rarely make direct sounds.

Instead:

Players hear:

- Metallic scraping
- Distant footsteps
- Low-frequency vibration
- Breathing (rare)

The uncertainty is more frightening than constant noise.

---

# Audio Priorities

1. Gameplay feedback
2. Environmental ambience
3. Narrative support
4. Music

Never sacrifice gameplay clarity.
